SDG 3 Prevention

Malaria Prevention in Sierra Leone

Malaria remains one of the leading causes of death in Sierra Leone. Using insecticide-treated bed nets, removing stagnant water, and seeking early treatment are the most effective prevention strategies.

Key Tips

  • Sleep under treated mosquito nets every night
  • Remove stagnant water sources around your home
  • Wear long sleeves at dusk and dawn
  • Visit a clinic immediately if you develop fever
Ministry of Health, SL March 2026
SDG 3 Maternal Health

Maternal & Child Health

Sierra Leone has one of the highest maternal mortality rates globally. Antenatal care, skilled birth attendants, and postnatal support are critical to saving mothers' and babies' lives.

Key Tips

  • Attend all 8 recommended antenatal care visits
  • Deliver in a health facility with skilled staff
  • Breastfeed exclusively for the first 6 months
  • Immunise your child on the national schedule
UNICEF Sierra Leone March 2026
SDG 6 Hygiene

Clean Water & Sanitation

Waterborne diseases like cholera and typhoid are entirely preventable. Access to clean water and proper sanitation practices save thousands of lives in Sierra Leone each year.

Key Tips

  • Boil or treat all drinking water before use
  • Wash hands with soap before eating and after using the toilet
  • Use latrines properly and maintain them clean
  • Keep food covered and stored safely away from flies
WHO Sierra Leone March 2026
SDG 3 Mental Health

Mental Health Awareness

Mental health is often overlooked in Sierra Leone. Post-conflict trauma, the Ebola legacy, poverty, and stigma create compounding barriers. Community support and awareness make a real difference.

Key Tips

  • Talk openly to someone you trust about your feelings
  • Seek help from a trained health worker or counsellor
  • Avoid self-medicating with alcohol or substances
  • Support community members who are visibly struggling
Mental Health Coalition SL April 2026
SDG 3 Prevention

HIV/AIDS Prevention

HIV/AIDS continues to affect thousands of Sierra Leoneans. Regular testing, consistent protection, and access to antiretroviral therapy are essential to halting the spread.

Key Tips

  • Get tested regularly — know your status
  • Use protection consistently with every partner
  • Know your partner's HIV status before sex
  • Seek antiretroviral therapy immediately if positive
NAS Sierra Leone April 2026
SDG 2 Nutrition

Childhood Nutrition

Malnutrition affects nearly one-third of children under five in Sierra Leone. A diverse, balanced diet rich in local vegetables, proteins, and fruits supports healthy growth and brain development.

Key Tips

  • Feed children diverse, locally available nutritious foods
  • Include iron-rich foods like beans, fish, and dark leaves
  • Monitor your child's growth at clinic check-ups
  • Supplement with Vitamin A where available
WFP Sierra Leone April 2026
